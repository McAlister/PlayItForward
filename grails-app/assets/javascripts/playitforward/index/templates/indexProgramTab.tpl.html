The Playmat Program

People leave competitive play when the downsides outweigh the upsides. The PiF initiative seeks to maximize upsides and minimize downsides through competition based community building. During large events we offer a unique playmat and recognition to the highest placing woman or non-binary player who makes day 2.


The existence of the competition creates an awareness in each individual that they are not alone. This reduces stereotype threat - the fear that other members of your group will be held accountable for your mistakes. The social network formed by it creates upsides in the form of friends that one can only see by continuing to participate in high level competitive play. It is also a resource for room sharing and logistic support.


Modo Zerg Rushes

Periodically PiF competitors are encouraged to get MODO tokens to all attend the same MCQ online. In one such event Sarah England went undefeated and qualified for the Pro Tour.


Real Life Zerg Rushes

Once per year a GP is designated for a big award - flight and hotel to your next GP. The top 8 PiF competitors in Day 2 cut to a draft for the prize (Unless they are in the real top 8 cause we aren't waiting for that to finish).
