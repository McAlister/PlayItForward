We are a non-profit with one goal:

Get more women on the Pro Tour

Play It Forward was inspired by a study showing that the rarity of women chess grandmasters was driven almost entirely (96%) by participation rates. Populations perform on a Bell curve with fewer and fewer at either extreme. Only 1 in 16 chess players are women so you see very few women players among the very best/worst chess players. The problem is much worse in Magic where fewer than 1 in 50 GP players are women. Play It Forward seeks to address the participation gap by focusing on player retention.


People leave competitive play when the downsides outweigh the upsides. When you are part of a marginalized group you have more downsides and fewer upsides so that point comes sooner. The PiF initiative seeks to maximize upsides and minimize downsides through competition based community building. During large events we offer a unique playmat and recognition to the highest placing woman or non-binary player who makes day 2. We also invite them to a private social network.


The existence of the competition creates an awareness in each individual that they are not alone. This helps participants shed the burden of what Chantelle Campbell dubbed The Mantle . The Mantle is knowing that other members of your group will be held accountable for your mistakes. Academia refers to it as "stereotype threat" and it is a negative stresser that non-trivially degrades both performance and enjoyment. The social network creates upsides - close friends that one can only see by continuing to participate in high level competitive play. It is also a resource for room sharing and logistic support. LoTG is 450 members strong and growing.


When we started in 2016 women were a little over half a percent of players at a typical GP. Participation rates have nearly doubled with a high point of 2.83% at GP Vegas. Women who get involved with PiF play more GP's per year than they previously did, place higher, and are less likely to leave competitive play. Non-binary players report significant improvements in quality of event experience. I would like to thank all the volunteers and donors who have made PiF possible.


Play It Forward is gamer run and is not affiliated with WoTC or tournament organizers.
